# AI Agency Objection Handling Guide

**Purpose:** Equip your sales team with the exact scripts needed to overcome the most common objections heard when selling AI and automation services.

---

## 1. "It's too expensive."

**The Psychology:** They either don't have the cash (unqualified), or you haven't proved that the ROI outweighs the cost.

**Rebuttal:**
"I completely understand. A $5,000 investment isn't pocket change. But let's look at the math we discussed earlier. You mentioned that missing 5 calls a week is costing you roughly $10,000 a month in lost revenue, plus you're paying an answering service $800 a month to just take messages. 

If we deploy this AI Voice Agent, it replaces that $800/mo cost entirely, and it starts capturing those missed leads immediately. This system pays for itself in less than 30 days. Are you opposed to making a one-time investment to permanently fix a $10,000/mo leak in your business?"

## 2. "I'm worried the AI will hallucinate or say the wrong thing to my customers."

**The Psychology:** Fear of brand damage. They've used ChatGPT and seen it act weird. 

**Rebuttal:**
"That is a completely valid concern, and it's exactly why you hire an agency instead of trying to build this yourself. When you use ChatGPT on your phone, it has the freedom to talk about anything. 

When we build an AI agent for your business, we put it in a 'walled garden.' We program strict guardrails so it can *only* answer questions based on the PDF documents and website links we feed it. If a customer asks a question it doesn't know, it is hard-coded to say, 'Let me transfer you to a human specialist.' We also do rigorous stress-testing for a full week before it ever speaks to a real customer. Does that ease your concern?"

## 3. "We just want to hire a Virtual Assistant (VA) to do this."

**The Psychology:** VAs are a known quantity; AI is new and scary.

**Rebuttal:**
"A VA is a great option for many tasks. However, let's look at your specific goal: responding to leads in under 60 seconds, 24/7. 
A VA needs to sleep, takes sick days, requires training, and can only handle one conversation at a time. If three leads come in at once, two are waiting. 

Our AI automation handles 1,000 simultaneous conversations, responds in 3 seconds, works 24/7/365, and never asks for a raise. Why pay a VA $1,500 every single month for a slower response time, when you can build the AI once?"

## 4. "Send me some more information / Send me a proposal and I'll think about it."

**The Psychology:** This is a polite brush-off. They are trying to get off the phone without committing.

**Rebuttal:**
"I'd be happy to send you a proposal, John. But just so I don't send you a generic PDF that wastes your time, what exactly are you hoping to see in that proposal that we haven't covered today?"

*(Wait for their answer, address the specific concern, then lock in the review call)*

"Makes sense. I'll include the exact technical breakdown of the CRM integration you asked about. Let's put 15 minutes on the calendar for Thursday to review that document together so I can answer any technical questions you have on the spot. Do you have your calendar open?"

## 5. "I need to talk to my business partner."

**The Psychology:** You failed to qualify the decision-makers at the start of the call.

**Rebuttal:**
"Of course, I wouldn't expect you to make a decision like this without your partner. Based on what we've discussed, are *you* fully on board with this solution?"
*(If Yes)*: "Great. Since your partner hasn't heard the pitch, they're going to have a lot of questions about the tech and the ROI that might be hard for you to answer second-hand. Let's schedule a 15-minute call for later this week with all three of us, so I can show them exactly what I just showed you."
