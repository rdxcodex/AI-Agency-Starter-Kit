# Business Discovery Questionnaire

**Purpose:** This document is meant to be used *internally* by the agency owner during a live discovery call. Do not send this to the client to fill out. Use it as a script/framework to uncover deep pain points and attach financial value to the problems you will solve.

---

## Stage 1: The Current State (Where are they now?)
*Goal: Understand how the business makes money and where it is currently breaking down.*

1. **"Can you walk me through the lifecycle of a customer in your business? From the moment they find you to the moment they pay?"**
   * *Notes:* [Listen for manual touchpoints, copy-pasting, human error, delays]
2. **"How many [leads/support tickets/appointments] are you currently processing per week?"**
   * *Notes:* [Establish the volume]
3. **"Where do things usually fall through the cracks?"**
   * *Notes:* [Identify the primary pain point]
4. **"Walk me through the exact step-by-step process your team takes when [insert specific pain point, e.g., a new lead comes in]?"**
   * *Notes:* [Map the manual workflow]

## Stage 2: The Pain and Financial Impact (Why does this matter?)
*Goal: Attach a dollar figure to their problem. If you can't prove it's costing them money, you can't sell a high-ticket solution.*

5. **"How many hours a week does your team spend doing [manual task]?"**
   * *Notes:* [Calculate: Hours * Employee Wage = Money Wasted]
6. **"What happens when a lead isn't followed up with immediately?"**
   * *Notes:* [Calculate: Lost deals * Average Order Value = Revenue Lost]
7. **"If your customer support team is overwhelmed, how is that impacting churn or repeat business?"**
   * *Notes:* [Identify secondary financial impacts]
8. **"So, it sounds like this inefficiency is costing you roughly $,000 every month. Is that an accurate assessment?"**
   * *Notes:* [Get verbal agreement on the cost of the problem]

## Stage 3: The Desired State (Where do they want to be?)
*Goal: Define the success criteria for your project.*

9. **"If we could wave a magic wand and fix this process, what does the perfect workflow look like to you?"**
   * *Notes:* [Define their dream scenario]
10. **"If we successfully automate this, what will your team do with the extra [X] hours per week?"**
    * *Notes:* [Shift focus from cost-savings to revenue-generation]
11. **"What is your target for [Leads/Sales/Resolution time] over the next 6 months?"**
    * *Notes:* [Align your solution with their growth goals]

## Stage 4: Qualification & Next Steps
*Goal: Ensure they have the budget and authority to buy.*

12. **"Who else is involved in making decisions regarding operational software and agency partners?"**
    * *Notes:* [Identify if you are speaking to the true Decision Maker]
13. **"We typically build solutions like this in the $[Minimum Engagement] to $[Average Engagement] range, depending on complexity. Is that within the realm of possibility for a solution that saves you $[Amount discussed in Stage 2]?"**
    * *Notes:* [Soft pitch the price. Watch their reaction.]
14. **"Great. Based on what you've told me, I'm confident we can help. I'm going to put together a detailed proposal outlining the scope, deliverables, and exact pricing. Can we schedule a time next [Day of week] to review it together?"**
    * *Notes:* [Never send a proposal without booking the review call first.]
