# Cold DM Templates (Twitter/X & Facebook)

**Purpose:** Direct messaging requires an extremely casual, zero-pressure approach. DMs that look like cold emails get blocked immediately. The goal here is to get a "hand raise."

---

## Template 1: The "Quick Question" (High Volume)
*Best for fast outreach on Twitter/X to agency owners, realtors, or coaches.*

**Message:**
"Hey John, love the content lately. Quick question—are you guys currently handling all your lead follow-up manually, or do you have some AI routing set up?"

*(If they reply: "Manually" or "What do you mean?")*

**Reply:**
"Got it. I actually build AI systems for Home Services that text new leads within 3 seconds so you don't lose them to competitors. Built a quick demo of how it works, want me to drop the link here?"

## Template 2: The Hyper-Personalized Loom (High Value)
*Best for high-ticket prospects where you've spent 5 minutes researching their business.*

**Message 1:**
"Hey John, was just checking out your site at https://example.com. Really clean brand."

*(Wait 2 minutes)*

**Message 2:**
"I actually noticed a quick bottleneck on your booking page that's probably costing you a few appointments a week. Recorded a 60-second Loom showing how to fix it with a simple automation. Cool if I send it over?"

## Template 3: The "Beta Tester" Angle
*Best when you are just starting out and need case studies, not cash.*

**Message:**
"Hey John, I'm a developer building a new AI Voice agent specifically for [Industry, e.g., Roofers]. It basically answers the phone and books estimates automatically. 

I'm looking for 2-3 businesses to test it out for free this month so I can get some case studies. You guys have a great reputation in Austin, would you be open to testing it? No cost."

## Important DM Rules:
1. **Never send a link in the first message.** Social media algorithms will filter it to the "Hidden" or "Spam" folder. Always ask for permission to send the link.
2. **Keep it under 3 sentences.** People read DMs on their phones while doing other things.
3. **Use lowercase letters.** It sounds counterintuitive, but typing casually (like you would to a friend) gets higher response rates in DMs than formal, perfectly punctuated corporate speak.
