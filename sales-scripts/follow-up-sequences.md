# Follow-Up Sequences (The "Money" Sequence)

**Purpose:** 80% of sales require 5 follow-ups after the initial contact. If a prospect expressed interest but went dark, use this sequence to revive the deal without being annoying.

---

## The Scenario:
You had a great discovery call. You sent the proposal. The prospect said they would "look it over." Now, it's been 4 days and they are ghosting you.

## Follow-Up 1: The Value Add (Day 3 of Ghosting)
*Goal: Bump the thread without saying "Just checking in." Provide a piece of value.*

**Subject:** Re: [Original Proposal Subject Line]

Hi John,

Hope you're having a good week. 

While you're reviewing the proposal, I actually saw this article/case-study this morning on how [Competitor Brand / General Industry] is using AI to [solve the exact problem you discussed]. Thought you might find it interesting given our conversation. 

Let me know if any questions popped up regarding the integration timeline we mapped out!

Best,
Alex Mercer

## Follow-Up 2: The Soft Check-In (Day 7)
*Goal: A low-pressure ping.*

Hi John,

Just bubbling this up to the top of your inbox. Let me know if you need me to clarify anything in the proposal or if we need to adjust the scope to better fit your timeline.

Cheers,
Alex Mercer

## Follow-Up 3: The "Permission to Close" / Breakup (Day 14)
*Goal: Trigger their loss aversion. This has the highest reply rate of any cold email.*

**Subject:** Close this out?

Hi John,

I haven't heard back from you, so I'm assuming solving the [Mention Pain Point, e.g., lead routing bottleneck] isn't a priority for this quarter. 

I'm going to go ahead and close out your file on our end so I stop cluttering your inbox. 

If this becomes a priority again in the future, feel free to reach back out! 

Best,
Alex Mercer

---

*(Note: 50% of the time, they will reply to the breakup email apologizing for being busy and ask to move forward. If they don't reply, actually stop emailing them and move on.)*
